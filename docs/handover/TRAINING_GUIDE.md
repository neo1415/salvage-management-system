# Training Guide

This guide supports the initial staff training session and can be adapted into a client-facing manual.

## Training Goals

By the end of training, users should be able to:

- Log in with the correct role.
- Complete their assigned salvage workflow without developer assistance.
- Understand how case, auction, payment, document, pickup, and reporting statuses connect.
- Know which dashboard or queue to check when work is blocked.
- Know how to escalate support, privacy, security, or data issues.

## Role 1: Claims Adjuster

Core responsibility: create accurate salvage cases and supporting evidence.

Training flow:

1. Log in as claims adjuster.
2. Open the adjuster dashboard.
3. Create a new case.
4. Add claim reference, asset details, market value, condition, mileage, photos, notes, and location.
5. Review AI assessment output and warnings.
6. Submit case for manager review.
7. Track case status after manager action.
8. Use reports to review case-processing performance.

Important rules:

- Photos and location evidence should be captured at the time of inspection where possible.
- AI assessment is advisory; adjuster and manager judgment remains final.
- Claim reference must match the insurer's source record.

## Role 2: Salvage Manager

Core responsibility: approve cases, manage auctions, review vendors/KYC, and control operational exceptions.

Training flow:

1. Review manager dashboard and Recovery Control Tower.
2. Review submitted cases.
3. Approve or reject cases.
4. Create/approve auctions.
5. Monitor auctions and exceptions.
6. Review KYC applications and evidence.
7. Approve/reject KYC with reasons.
8. Review pickup queues and vendor performance.
9. Use reports for operational oversight.

Important rules:

- Reject actions should include clear reasons.
- Auction policy and vendor tier controls should be reviewed before launch.
- KYC provider evidence should be treated as decision support, not as a substitute for review.

## Role 3: Finance Officer

Core responsibility: verify payments, monitor settlement exposure, and reconcile funds.

Training flow:

1. Open finance dashboard.
2. Review Settlement Control.
3. Review payment list and filters.
4. Verify payment details and references.
5. Confirm payment exceptions and overdue items.
6. Review reconciliation alerts.
7. Confirm payment-to-pickup handoff state.
8. Export payment/revenue reports when needed.

Important rules:

- Verify payment source and reference before approval.
- Reconciliation discrepancies should be escalated immediately.
- Registration fees and auction payments should be reviewed separately.

## Role 4: System Admin

Core responsibility: manage users, policies, privacy requests, fraud queues, and system health.

Training flow:

1. Open admin dashboard.
2. Review system health and operations control.
3. Manage users and roles.
4. Review fraud alerts.
5. Review pickup confirmations.
6. Manage privacy/data-right requests.
7. Review audit logs.
8. Confirm business policy and notification settings.

Important rules:

- Role changes must follow internal approval.
- Privacy requests should be handled within the documented policy timeline.
- Fraud alerts require review notes before dismissal/confirmation.

## Role 5: Vendor / Buyer

Core responsibility: register, complete KYC, bid, sign documents, pay, and complete pickup.

Training flow:

1. Register/log in.
2. Complete BVN/Tier 1 verification if required.
3. Pay registration fee if configured.
4. Submit Tier 2 KYC if required.
5. Browse eligible auctions.
6. Place bid and complete OTP if required.
7. If selected as winner, sign required documents.
8. Complete payment.
9. Follow pickup authorization and confirmation process.
10. Review dashboard, bid history, documents, and notifications.

Important rules:

- Bidding access depends on verification tier and business policy.
- Payment and pickup deadlines affect vendor reliability score.
- Vendors should keep notification channels active.

## Training Exercise

Run one complete sandbox workflow:

1. Adjuster creates case.
2. Manager approves case.
3. Auction is created and opened.
4. Vendor bids and wins.
5. Documents are generated and signed.
6. Payment is verified.
7. Pickup is confirmed by vendor and staff.
8. Finance and manager reports are reviewed.
9. Audit log is checked.

## Support and Escalation

Operational issues:

- Capture screenshot.
- Record user role, page URL, action attempted, and timestamp.
- Check dashboard exception queues.
- Escalate to system admin or developer support.

Security/privacy issues:

- Do not share sensitive documents over chat.
- Use protected document links.
- Escalate suspected account compromise, payment fraud, or data exposure immediately.
